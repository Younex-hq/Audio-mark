# AudioMark

Take structured notes with timestamps directly on your audio files — fully local and privacy-first.

## Why AudioMark Exists

Tools like NotebookLM can generate high-quality audio overviews. However, they lack core listening workflows:

* You cannot mark precise timestamps.
* You cannot take structured notes while listening.
* You cannot easily resume from where you stopped with your annotations.

AudioMark solves this.

It allows you to:

* Add timestamped markers while listening
* Write structured notes
* Export and import your annotations
* Resume exactly where you left off

Everything runs locally in your browser. No uploads. No tracking. No servers.

---

## Use Cases

AudioMark is useful when:

* You recorded a meeting and need timestamped notes
* You are reviewing an AI-generated audio overview
* You are listening to an audiobook and want structured highlights
* You are studying from recorded lectures
* You are reviewing interviews or podcasts

---

## Core Features

* Local-only processing (privacy-first)
* Timestamped markers
* Free-form note taking
* Export annotations as `.json`
* Import saved annotations
* Resume listening sessions

---

## How It Works

### 1. Upload Your Audio

Upload any supported audio file directly into the app.

### 2. Listen and Annotate

While listening:

* Add timestamp markers
* Write notes linked to specific moments

### 3. Export Your Notes

Export annotations as a human-readable `.json` file.

The format is portable and easy to:

* Store
* Version control
* Transform into other formats later

### 4. Resume Anytime

When you return:

* Re-upload the same audio file
* Import your saved `.json`
* Continue exactly where you stopped

---

## Important Note

Because the application runs entirely in your browser:

* It does not store file paths.
* When you close the page, the audio file must be selected again.
* Your annotations remain available on your browser.

This is a deliberate design decision to guarantee privacy.
